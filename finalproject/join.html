Basic

Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and basic phrases aimed at satisfying concrete needs. Can introduce themselves and others, ask and answer questions about personal details (e.g., where they live, people they know, things they have). Communication is limited to simple and routine tasks requiring a simple and direct exchange of information on familiar topics.

Intermediate

Can understand the main ideas of complex texts on both concrete and abstract topics related to their field of expertise. Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity that makes regular interaction with native speakers quite possible without strain for either party. Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects related to their interests.

Advanced

Can produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects related to their field of interest. Can express themselves fluently and spontaneously without much obvious searching for expressions. Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes. Can produce well-organized and clearly articulated speech and writing.

Proficient

Can read with ease virtually all forms of the written language, including abstract, structurally complex, or highly colloquial texts. Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations. Has a high level of accuracy and can adapt their language use to different contexts and audiences. Their understanding of language nuances is at near-native level.
